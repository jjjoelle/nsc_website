Starting the NSC program can feel overwhelming. You're surrounded by people from different backgrounds — physicists, biologists, computer scientists, engineers — and the coursework spans all of it. Here's what I wish someone had told me on day one.

## Course Selection

Don't try to take everything. The INI courses are excellent, but you'll burn out if you overload your first semester. I'd recommend:

- **Neuroinformatics** (mandatory and foundational)
- One computational course (Neural Systems or Computational Models)
- One experimental/methods course

Leave room for the unexpected. My favorite course was one I added in week two after sitting in on a lecture.

## Finding Your Lab

Start visiting labs early. Most PIs are happy to chat over coffee. Don't wait until the short project deadline to figure out what you're interested in — you'll end up rushing into something that doesn't fit.

## The Social Side

The NSC cohort is small, which is both a strength and a challenge. Make an effort to get to know people outside your immediate study group. The Thursday aperos at INI are a great place to start.

## One More Thing

Zurich is expensive. The Mensa at Irchel is your friend. And the hike up to Uetliberg is mandatory at least once per semester.
